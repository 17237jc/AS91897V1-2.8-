Image Region named after Māori History of each Taumata

The Tararua Taumata is named after the Tararuas Ranges in the lower North Island of New Zealand. The Tararua Range was named after important Ngāti Toa chief, Te Rangihaeata, to commemorate a peace arrangement between the Ngāti Toa and Ngāti Kahungunu tribes.

The Pukeariki Taumata is named after the West Mountains and Hills in the Lower Hutt Region. Puke ariki means the hill of chiefs. It represents the site of a Māori pā of Te Āti Awa tribe and the Chief Te Rangi-apiti-rua.

The Remutaka Taumata is named after the East Mountain ranges that extend in the lower North Island from Wellington to Wairarapa. A Maori Ancestor named Haunui-a-nanaia named the mountains Remutaka which means ‘remu’ to ‘gaze and look’ and ‘taka’, ‘to sit down’.

The Pukeatua Taumata is named after the Wainuiomata and Waiwhetu Hills. It means Hills of the Gods which represents the sacred significance and the history of the mountains. The Bridge at the top of the Wainuiomata Hill is also named Pukeatua which recognises the importance of the hill connecting the Hutt Valley to the Maori Ancestry of Wainuiomata.

The Matairangi Taumata is named after a legend of two taniwhas who tried to escape from Wellington harbour which used to be an enclosed lake. Maitairangi is a hill called Mount Victoria in Wellington. Its original name was Tangi Te Keo. One taniwha died and its spirit became a bird named Te Keo which flew to the top of this mountain and mourned which means Tangi.

The Orongorongo Taumata is named after the Orongorongo valley and river in Wainuiomata. It is a shortened version of Te Tai O Rongorongo whichs means the water of Rongorongo who was the wife of Chief Turi Kaihautu. Rongo also means the Maori God of Agriculture. The River flows 32km in the Southern Remutaka Ranges.
